Understanding TFBS Evaluation Metrics

Learn how we evaluate and score Transcription Factor Binding Sites (TFBS) in our database.

Introduction

Our database uses two key metrics to evaluate TFBS regions: the Confident Score and the Important Score. These scores help researchers assess the reliability and biological significance of predicted TFBS regions.

Confident Score

What is the Confident Score?

The Confident Score measures the reliability of a TFBS prediction, indicating how confident we are that the region represents a true transcription factor binding site.

How to Use These Scores

When analyzing TFBS regions, consider both scores together:

  • • High Confident Score + High Important Score: Strong candidate for experimental validation
  • • High Confident Score + Low Important Score: Reliable prediction but may be less biologically relevant
  • • Low Confident Score + High Important Score: May warrant further investigation despite lower confidence
  • • Low Confident Score + Low Important Score: May be less reliable or significant
